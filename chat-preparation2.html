Virtual EMDR Addiction Protocol


The word "Addiction" is derived from Latin for "enslaved by." It aptly describes the hopelessness of being enslaved to your addiction, powerless to break free.


Whatever the Addiction - whether to substances (drugs, alcohol, tobacco) or harmful habits (gambling, sex, food, work) - addiction is difficult to overcome. Its roots are complex, often interlinked with trauma or childhood issues.


Virtual EMDR works to change how your brain responds to Addiction.

Using Virtual EMDR, you will feel like someone has turned down the volume on your addiction. Your cravings and triggers will begin to fade, become more manageable, and eventually disappear.

Take some time to write out how your Addiction manifests in your life. This will give you valuable insights to use in your session.

  • Have you tried to stop previously? What happened?
  • How motivated are you to stop now? Why?
  • What are you most afraid of if you do not change? If you DO change?

Virtual EMDR PTSD & Trauma Protocol


Traumatic events may relate to witnessing or being involved in crime, war, natural disasters, accidents, and abuse (such as physical, sexual, emotional, mental). They can be a single-incident event, or a series of related events over a period of time.


When Traumatic Events occur, the brain is not able to process these memories normally, leading to the memories replaying endlessly in your mind, and PTSD symptoms such as difficulty sleeping and nightmares, problems concentrating, avoiding places, people, and activities that are reminders of the trauma, and being easily irritated and angered.

Using EMDR, you will feel like someone has turned down the volume on your Trauma and PTSD Symptoms. The difficult feelings will begin to fade, become more manageable, and eventually disappear.

Virtual EMDR Grief & Loss Protocol


When you suffer a Loss - such as the death of a loved one, miscarriage, divorce or separation, or even the loss of a life dream or expectation - it is natural to grieve for that loss.


Normally Grief resolves itself naturally over time. You will may still feel sadness and longing when you think of what you lost, but life resumes normally for the most part.


But sometimes Grief can be so intense that it cannot be resolved normally. You may experience vivid nightmares, recurring flashbacks, deep sorrow, or fantasies about your loved one even years later. You may also struggle with feelings of responsibility and guilt, or unsettling fears about yourself or others around you.


If unresolved Grief is preventing you from getting back to living life, the Virtual EMDR Grief Program can help.

Through using EMDR, the difficult feelings associated with your Grief will begin to fade, and over time, be replaced with positive empowering beliefs, acceptance and hope.

Depression effects millions of people world-wide, yet it continues to be often overlooked as "just feeling sad."


If you are experiencing symptoms of Depression - such as overpowering feelings of hopelessness or worthlessness, loss of interest in activities, difficulty eating and sleeping, or thoughts of death, Virtual EMDR can help you feel better.

Using EMDR, you will feel like someone has turned down the volume on your Depression symptoms. EMDR helps you overcome Depression by re-programming the negative thoughts in your brain, and replacing them with positive, empowering beliefs.

Virtual EMDR Phobia & Fears Protocol


A Phobia is an intense Fear triggered by an object (such as insects or animals) or situation (such as flying, public speaking, driving, or heights) which is out of proportion to the actual danger posed.


Living with a Phobia or Fear is difficult. Well-meaning friends and family often do not understand: they may make fun or even "help" you get over the Phobia by exposing you intentionally to it.


In severe cases, Fears and Phobias can impact your career or relationships, and may lead to PTSD.


Working through EMDR Sessions, will make it feel like the volume is turned down on your Fear and Phobia Symptoms. The difficult feelings of stress and anxiety will begin to diminish, become more controllable, and then disappear.
